Hydrogen Reformer System

A modular reactor system that converts LPG and water into hydrogen through high-temperature catalysis. The hydrogen is then used to generate electricity via a fuel cell. The project involved reactor design, selecting high-temperature metals and seals, specifying sensors, creating automation/control systems, and performing thermodynamic, fluid mechanic, and fatigue analyses.

Specific client details remain confidential, but the system provided complex engineering challenges and valuable learning experiences.

What I Did

  • Detailed reactor design and CAD modeling
  • CFD simulations and thermodynamic analysis
  • Component and sensor specification
  • Fatigue, compression, and sealing calculations
  • Automation and PLC control
  • Assembly, leak testing, welding, and commissioning
  • Design for manufacturability
  • Project management and client liaison
